Note on Billings vs Revenue (Critical for Fortinet)

Unlike pure-SaaS peers (CRWD, NET), Fortinet reports both revenue (GAAP, deferred-revenue-adjusted) and billings (cash invoiced to customers; Fortinet's preferred forward-looking indicator). Billings have been more volatile than revenue: FY23 +15%, FY24 only +2% (as the post-pandemic firewall refresh cycle paused), then re-accelerated to +16% in FY25. Q4 FY25 billings grew +18% YoY ($2.37B), well above the high end of guidance. The Unified SASE pillar is the standout: Q4 SASE billings +40% YoY, FortiSASE ARR +90%; SecOps ARR $491M (+21%); Unified SASE ARR $1.28B (+11%). Combined SASE+SecOps now represent 36% of total billings (up from 30% two years ago). The "Rule of 45" (revenue growth + non-GAAP op margin) was achieved for the 6th consecutive year in FY25 (~14% + 35.5% = 49.5%).

⑥ Invert — Charlie Munger Style

Why This Could Be a Bad Long-Term Investment

The bear case for Fortinet is structural, not cyclical. The cybersecurity industry is consolidating around platforms that integrate AI-native operations (PANW Cortex XSIAM, CRWD Falcon, MSFT Defender). Anthropic's Project Glasswing — by elevating PANW and CRWD as "AI enforcement layer" partners while excluding Fortinet — has cemented a perception that Fortinet is on the wrong side of the AI/cloud-native divide. Fortinet's strengths — ASIC-based hardware performance, integrated FortiOS for branch security, sovereign-SASE deployment — are genuinely valuable but for an installed base that is aging in profile: distributed enterprises with on-premise data centres, OT/industrial sites, government/sovereign deployments. As more workload migrates to cloud-native architectures, Fortinet's hardware moat (FortiASIC) loses relevance. The 55% firewall unit-share statistic management cites is itself a backward-looking metric — measured in units sold, not workloads protected. A single PANW Prisma deployment in a public cloud may protect 1000x the workloads of a small-branch FortiGate appliance, but each counts as one "unit." If unit-share is the moat, the moat is shrinking faster than the absolute number suggests.

What Could Invalidate the Bull Thesis Despite Good Financials

A world where Fortinet's billings growth re-decelerates to single-digits in FY27-28 — even with FCF holding steady — would prove that the FY25 +16% billings growth was a refresh-cycle peak, not a sustainable run-rate. The signal would be: SASE + SecOps combined growth slowing below 20% (currently +24% combined for FY25), which would imply the platform thesis is failing to expand wallet share at existing customers. The other invalidation path is a high-profile FortiOS vulnerability that a Project Glasswing partner (CRWD or PANW using Claude Mythos) discovers and the media frames as "Fortinet's Achilles heel." Fortinet has historically had a higher rate of disclosed CVEs than peers (partly because of its broader product portfolio, partly because of disclosure cadence) — in an AI-vulnerability-discovery world this risk is heightened. Any major government or large-enterprise security incident attributed to a FortiGate would do disproportionate damage to the brand vs the cloud-native peers.

Assumptions That Must Go Right for This to Succeed

The DCF Base scenario at $85 (r=9%) requires: (1) Sovereign SASE materialising as Ken Xie's pitch — Fortinet captures meaningful share of sovereign cloud deployments in Europe (GDPR/data-residency), Middle East (Saudi/UAE), India (RBI/MEITY), and Japan that the public-cloud SASE players cannot serve. (2) FortiSASE momentum sustaining — ARR currently +90% YoY needs to compound to $3-4B by FY28 to support the SASE+SecOps growth narrative. (3) SecOps re-acceleration — quarterly billings must rebound from Q4's +6% back toward the FY25 +22% pace, validating that Fortinet can compete with CRWD Falcon in the SOC. (4) No major FortiOS CVE incident in FY26-27 that becomes a brand catalyst (historically Fortinet has been hit by widely-publicised vulnerabilities — VPN auth bypass, FortiManager command injection — and these always pressure the stock). (5) Margin stability through the memory-cost cycle — non-GAAP op margin must stay within the guided 33-36% band. All five need to broadly hold. Any two failing simultaneously likely pushes intrinsic value toward the Bear/Base-Low range of $59-79.

Structural Weaknesses — The Munger Pre-Mortem

1. Hardware exposure in a software-defined world: ~33% of Fortinet's revenue is product (hardware), and even much of the "service" revenue is tied to FortiGuard subscriptions sold alongside hardware. As enterprises shift to cloud-native zero-trust architectures (CRWD, NET, ZS), the per-customer dollar opportunity for Fortinet shrinks even if the customer count grows. 2. Founder/family concentration: Ken Xie (CEO) and Michael Xie (President, CTO) are brothers; founders own 15.79% of stock. Power and succession planning rest on two related individuals. Ken is 62; Michael is 60. There is no clear successor outside the family. 3. Anthropic Glasswing exclusion is an information signal: Anthropic chose CSCO + PANW + CRWD as cybersecurity insiders. Fortinet was not chosen. This may reflect either (a) Anthropic's enterprise sales team's relative familiarity, or (b) a substantive judgment about which platforms are best positioned for AI-native security. Either way, the exclusion is a soft moat-erosion signal that Fortinet must address. 4. Multiple-compression risk: Fortinet historically traded at 38x forward earnings; today at 28x it has compressed but remains above historical floor of ~22x (2018-19). If sentiment continues to deteriorate, multiple could drift to 22x — implying ~$66 share price even if FY26 EPS hits $2.97 midpoint guidance.

Golden Tip 1 — The Cleanest GAAP-to-Non-GAAP Bridge in Tech

Fortinet's GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap is just ~4.5 percentage points (35.5% non-GAAP op margin vs 31.0% GAAP) — among the cleanest in cybersecurity. Compare to PANW (~16 pts), CRWD (~26 pts), NET (~22 pts). This means Fortinet's GAAP earnings are real, not financial-engineering theatre. Peter Lynch would insist on GAAP, and Fortinet's GAAP earnings tell almost the same story as its non-GAAP. The other peers' Glasswing wins (PANW, CRWD) come with the cost of their massive SBC burden — a hidden tax on shareholders that Fortinet's investors don't pay.

Golden Tip 2 — Founder Alignment is Rare at $62B Market Cap

Insider ownership of 15.79% at $62B market cap is exceptional — implies founders Ken Xie and Michael Xie hold roughly $9-10B of stock between them. For comparison: PANW insiders own 0.7% ($840M), CRWD insiders own 1.65% ($1.7B), NET insiders own ~3% ($300-400M). Lynch loved owner-operator companies — Fortinet is one of the cleanest examples in mature tech.

Golden Tip 3 — The "Rule of 45" is Genuinely Distinctive

Fortinet has hit "Rule of 45+" (revenue growth + non-GAAP op margin ≥ 45) for 6 consecutive years, with FY25 at ~49.5 (14% growth + 35.5% margin). For perspective: CRM is at ~44 (10% + 34%), PANW is at ~42 (14% + 28% non-GAAP), CRWD is at ~45 (21% + 24% non-GAAP) but with negative GAAP op margins, and NET is at ~42 (30% + ~13% non-GAAP) but with negative GAAP. The Rule of 45 is meaningful because it captures BOTH growth AND profitability — most software companies trade durability for growth or vice versa. Fortinet's 6-year streak of hitting both makes it a distinctive compounder. Management has reaffirmed the midterm "Rule of 45-plus" target. If FY26 hits ~12% revenue growth + 34.5% non-GAAP op margin = 46.5, the streak extends to 7. This is a hidden quality indicator that screening tools often miss because they look at only growth or only margin in isolation.

Golden Tip 4 — Sovereign SASE is the Asymmetric Bet

Most of the cybersecurity bull case for Fortinet is incremental: keep growing 12-14%, keep buying back stock, keep margins stable. But there is one asymmetric upside: Sovereign SASE. Most public cloud SASE players (Zscaler, Cloudflare, PANW Prisma) cannot deploy in a customer's own data centre — their architecture is fundamentally cloud-multitenant. Fortinet uniquely can deploy SASE in three modes: pure public cloud (FortiSASE), hybrid, OR fully on-premise/sovereign (the customer or service provider runs the SASE stack). With GDPR (EU), data localisation laws in India, China, Russia, and increasingly the Middle East, the sovereign-SASE TAM could be 50-100% of the public-SASE TAM by 2030 — and Fortinet has essentially no peer competitor in the on-prem deployment mode. Ken Xie's claim that "Sovereign SASE market is probably even bigger than the current public SASE market" is testable over 2026-27. If sovereign-SASE billings start being broken out and growing 50%+ YoY, this is the optionality that the Bull DCF scenario (16% Y1-3 FCF growth → $99-120 intrinsic) is implicitly pricing.
